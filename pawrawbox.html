PawRawBox

I recently started working with my friends over at PawRawBox, a company that provides raw pet food delivery. The company focuses on bringing convenience to dog owners who have little time for grocery shopping.

Challenges

To create a website that clearly displays subscription that fit the lifestyles of our target market who have busy schedules. The company needed a platform that allows users to quickly place subscription orders with a vast variety and combinations of order options.

My Role
  • User Experience Design
  • User Interface Design
  • Visual Design
  • Information Architecture
  • Wireframes
  • Prototypes
  • HTML5/CSS3
  • Responsive
Branding

The logo was designed for printing and web. I kept it simple and bold for easy recognition. At first, I was hoping to incorporate a box logo since it is a subscription ‘box’ business. After numerous of iterations, I decided to replace the “o” in the word ‘box’ with a paw to illustrate raw pet food in a box.

Research

Before designing the website, we researched the types of information we need to obtain from customers in order to match their subscription preferences. This helped me understood what options I should include on the site and organized them in a way that will be easy to choose from.

Sketches

We decided to use a one page website to tell customers what PawRawBox is, what services the company provides and how to get the services. Users could learn the three steps to subscription seamlessly, without having to navigate through pages.

Final

The responsive website was designed with mobile-first in mind.
